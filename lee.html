   Lee Chong Wei

Lee Chong Wei (Malaysia): is the best Malaysian badminton player of all time. No player in Malaysia has been that successful ever.
Lee was ranked 1st for 199 weeks in a row, from August from 21 August 2008 to 14 June 2012.

Here is the list of his trophies:

Five times Commonwealth champion;
Two times Asian championship winner;
One time Southeast Asia champion;
Three times silver medalist in the Olympic Games;
Three silver medals and one bronze medal in the World Championships.

Bibliography:

  • Lee Chong Wei's Achievements and Awards:

    He won bronze in the 2000 Asian Junior Championships in Kyoto, Japan & another bronze at World Junior Championships in Guangzhou, China, in 2000.
    Lee won the bronze in 2003, 2005, 2015 & bagged gold in the 2005 Southeast Asian Games.
    Lee Chong Wei won the Malaysia Open Champion Title twelve times from 2004-2006, 2008-2014, 2016, 2018.
    He was a gold medalist in the Commonwealth games in 2006, 2010 & 2018, respectively.
    Honoured with the Penang Sportsman Award eight times: 2005, 2007, 2008, 2009, 2010, 2011, 2012, 2016
    Bestowed with the National Sportsman Award in 2005, 2008, 2011, 2012
    Awarded with the TYT Prime Award Trophy six times: 2008, 2010, 2012, 2013, 2015 & 2016.
    He has also been awarded the Olympian of the Year Award three times: 2008, 2012 & 2016.
    Lee got awarded the BWF Player of the Year award five times: 2009, 2010, 2011, 2013 & 2016.
    In 2008, he was awarded the Sportswriters Association of Malaysia (SAM) Award.
    He was awarded the RTM's Most Popular Icon on Television Award in 2013.

  • Honours:

    In 2006, Lee has conferred Member of the Order of the Defender of the Realm.
    Lee Chong Wei was bestowed with the ‘Dato’ title by the Governor of Penang, Abdul Rahman Abbas.
    After Lee’s achievement at the 2016 Summer Olympics, he got promoted to the rank of Commander (Honorary).
    In 2016, Chong was bestowed with the title of "Datuk Wira" from Malacca Governor Mohd Khalil Yaakob.
    He was made the Commander of the Order of the Meritorious service, which carries the title‘Datuk’by Sultan Muhammad V of Malaysia in 2017.
    In Dec 2021, Lee Chong Wei received an honorary doctorate in sports science from Syed Sirajuddin Jamalullail, the chancellor of the Science University of Malaysia.
